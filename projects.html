Optimization of Complex Systems

State-of-the-art electronic devices are typically complex in their structures and features. For example, typical OLEDs consist of more than 5 layers in which the number of layers, materials and thicknesses can all be tuned. When additional components such as outcoupling layers are introduced, the engineering of these systems becomes high-dimensional in terms of number of paramters we can control. Yet, most of these parameters must be considered properly to devise the most efficient, functional, task-appropriate system. My biggest research interest is creating methodologies to address a given optimization problem most adequetly and efficiently. In these works, different ML-based techniques, mainly Bayesian optimization, were integrated in order to achieve optimization of complex systems.

Sub-Projects

  • Multi-objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs
  • Bayesian Optimization for OLED Engineering
  • k-NN-based Surrogate Model for Black-box Constrained Bayesian Optimization
  • Transformer-based Model for Prediction of Geometry Generation Failure in LightTools
  • Largely Fabricable Paraboloidal Lens for High-directional OLEDs
