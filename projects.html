Optimization of Complex Systems

Modern electronic and photonic devices often exhibit highly complex structures. Typical OLEDs, for example, contain multiple functional layers—each with tunable material choices and thicknesses— and may incorporate additional components such as light outcoupling structures. As these design spaces grow, the number of controllable parameters increases dramatically, making the resulting optimization problems high-dimensional, expensive, and non-convex.

My research focuses on developing methodologies for data-efficient optimization in such complex engineering systems. In the projects below, I integrate machine learning-based approaches to accelerate the search for high-performance device designs while reducing computational cost.

Sub-Projects

  • Failure-Aware Bayesian Optimization for OLED-MLA Design
  • Multi-Objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs

ML Works

My work in machine learning focuses on developing data-efficient models and training strategies that enhance the reliability of scientific design pipelines. I explore transformer-based architectures, mixture-of-experts (MoE) models, and reinforcement-learning-based data curation to improve prediction accuracy, reduce computational overhead, and support complex simulation-driven optimization tasks.

Sub-Projects

  • Data-Curated, Sparsely-Activated MoE for Spline Failure Prediction
  • Transformer-based MoE Classifier for Simulation Gating

Phototherapeutic Device Engineering

Wearable phototherapeutic devices provide a convenient and effective approach for treating conditions that respond to light exposure at specific wavelengths. Because these devices operate in close proximity to the epidermis, their optical output must be designed with great care to maximize therapeutic efficacy while preventing adverse effects.

My research in this area focuses on developing high-fidelity simulation tools that model photon transport in diffusion layers. These tools enable performance evaluation and safety validation prior to fabrication, reducing development cost and guiding optimal device design.

Sub-Projects

  • Photon-Transport Simulation for Phototherapeutic Device Diffusion Layer
  • Clinical Effect of Blue-Suppressed Light Source
