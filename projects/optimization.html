Optimization of Complex Systems

This page describes my work on optimization of complex electronic and photonic systems, with a focus on OLED micro-lens arrays (MLAs) for light outcoupling enhancement. The design spaces involved are high-dimensional and expensive to evaluate, motivating the use of Bayesian optimization to search for globally optimal device structures.

Sub-Projects

  • Failure-Aware Bayesian Optimization for OLED-MLA Design
  • Multi-objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs

Failure-Aware Bayesian Optimization for OLED-MLA Design

Micro-lens arrays (MLAs) are widely used to enhance OLED light outcoupling due to their effectiveness and planar compatibility. Although hemispherical lenses are a conventional choice for MLA unit structures, they are often sub-optimal when the full design space is considered.

In this work, I formulate the MLA design problem as a high-dimensional Bayesian optimization task. The unit lens shape and key geometric parameters are parameterized in a 12-dimensional design space, and optical simulations are used to evaluate device performance (e.g., external quantum efficiency and angular emission profiles). The goal is to identify MLA designs that significantly outperform the hemispherical baseline while respecting fabrication and physical constraints.

To handle simulation failures and invalid geometries, I employ a failure-aware optimization framework that explicitly accounts for spline-generation failures in the optical design software. This allows the optimizer to focus evaluations on valid and promising regions of the design space, improving data-efficiency and robustness of the search.

Using this framework, an OLED-MLA structure that achieves 28% enhancement in current efficiency compared to conventional hemispherical MLAs were discovered.

Multi-objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs

Prior to the work above, I developed a genetic-algorithm (GA)-based optimization tool to design OLED structures with enhanced light outcoupling. The algorithm jointly optimizes the MLA geometry and layer thicknesses in the device stack.

As a single-objective instance, the external quantum efficiency (EQE) was maximized and achieved up to 62.7%, approximately +3% improvement compared to conventional hemispherical MLAs.

The GA framework was further extended to handle multi-objective optimization, enabling simultaneous improvement of multiple performance metrics (e.g., EQE, angular uniformity, or viewing-angle constraints). I implemented both Pareto front-based methods and penalty function-based algorithms to generate trade-off solutions across conflicting objectives, providing designers with a spectrum of high-quality device configurations rather than a single point optimum.
