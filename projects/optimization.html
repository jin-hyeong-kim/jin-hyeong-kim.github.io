Multi-objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs

MLA (Micro-lens Arrays) are one of the most common methods used to enhance OLED efficiency due to their effectiveness and planar integrity.
Although hemisphere is the conventional choice for the unit lens structure, it is actually a sub-optimal solution.
Running accurate optical simulations within a well-designed optimization framework can give the global optimum given the emission material.
(picture of system) Here, the optimization of one or more desired OLED characteristics are defined as a 12-dimensional optimization problem.
The problem was solved by algorithms based on GA (genetic algorithm) which returns the optimal lens structure and layer thicknesses.
As a single-objective instance, EQE (external quantum efficiency) was maximized up to 62.7%. (+3% compared to hemispherical MLAs)
(EQE figure) The GA-based algorithm was slightly modified to be able to optimize multiple objectives at once.
Pareto solution-based methods and penalty function-based algorithms were developed separately to serve this purpose.
