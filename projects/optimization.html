Multi-objective Optimization Tool for Light Outcoupling Enhancement in OLEDs

MLA (Micro-lens Arrays) are one of the most common methods used to enhance OLED efficiency due to their effectiveness and planar integrity.
Although hemisphere is the conventional choice for the unit lens structure, it is actually a sub-optimal solution.
Running accurate optical simulations within a well-designed optimization framework can give the global optimum given the emission material.

Here, the optimization of one or more desired OLED characteristics are defined as a 12-dimensional optimization problem.
The problem was solved by algorithms based on GA (genetic algorithm) which returns the optimal lens structure and layer thicknesses.
As a single-objective instance, EQE (external quantum efficiency) was maximized up to 62.7%. (+3% compared to hemispherical MLAs)
The GA-based algorithm was slightly modified to be able to optimize multiple objectives at once.
Pareto solution-based methods and penalty function-based algorithms were developed separately to serve this purpose.

Bayesian Optimization for OLED Engineering

Optimizing complex systems such as the OLED+MLA often require more efficient methods than evolutionary algorithms.
To this end, I have worked on applying Bayesian methods for high-dimensional optimization to design OLEDs with free-form lens structures.
Bayesian optimization enables efficient exploration of complex, high-dimensional design spaces where traditional methods are costly or infeasible.
Specific aspects of the OLED engineering problem had to be addressed for this framework to function properly and efficiently. (See k-NN-based Surrogate Model for Black-box Constrained Bayesian Optimization for details)
CE (current efficiency) was maximized up to +12% of hemispherical MLAs using this framework.
The fabication of corresponding lens structure can be done utilizing a high-resolution printing techinque (2-photon polymerization)(2PP).

k-NN-based Surrogate Model for Black-box Constrained Bayesian Optimization

One of the defined problem's features is that failing conditions for inputs are unknown.
This is due to the underlying black-box geometric constraints within the Monte Carlo method-based optic simulation program LightToolsTM.
As a result, even input parameters that satisfy preliminary feasibility checks may ultimately fail during simulation, returning invalid outcomes.
This characteristic situates the problem as a black-box constrained optimization, where the objective is an expensive-to-evaluate black-box function and the constraints are also analytically intractable.
To address this challenge, we develop a k-NN-based surrogate model tailored for such settings.
The surrogate not only approximates the objective but also accounts for uncertainty in feasibility by learning directly from observed outcomes.
A warm-start initialization with well-distributed samples is employed to stabilize the surrogate training and ensure broad coverage of the design space during early optimization.
By integrating these elements, this framework achieves efficient exploration while maintaining robustness against unknown and probabilistic constraint violations.

Transformer-based Model for Prediction of Geometry Generation Failure in LightToolsTM

To provide an alternative way to address black-box geometric failure during the optical simulation process, I developed a transformer-based machine learning model that predicts when geometry generation in LightToolsTM will fail, given a set of input coordinates.
The model achieves over 95% accuracy, enabling researchers to avoid costly failed runs and focus computational resources on feasible designs.
Model accuracy is continuously being enhanced through feature engineering, hyperparameter optimization, and systematic evaluation of transformer architectures.
The incorporation of this predictive model not only accelerates Bayesian optimization for OLED engineering but also improves the robustness of simulation-driven design pipelines.

Largely Fabricable Paraboloidal Lens for High-directional OLEDs

Optimized lens structure in the works regarding OLEDs can be hard and costly to fabricate for some unit lens structure.
To tackle this, the Bayesian optimization framework was used to optimize unit lens structure in the design space of paraboloidal lenses.
Optimized paraboloidal lens can show improvement in directionality. (objective function set as power transmitted within small angles from the normal direction)
The corresponding lens can be easily fabricated in large-scale using 3D diffusion lithography.
